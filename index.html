Male suicide rates are double those of females over the world!

The number of suicide cases reached the highest in the age 35-54 years old in 2009, with over 90,000 cases, but in the year 2008 has the lowest at around 1640 cases, which is in the age of 5-14 years old. The number of suicides in the age 35-54 years old has nearly fifty-five times the amount of suicides in the age 5-14 years old.

The number of suicides in each age group for each year is shown by a bar. Every year, there are an increasing number of suicides in the United States. Russia, on the other hand, is steadily declining. Suicide rates are still fluctuating in Japan, the Republic of Korea, and Ukraine.

In the age of 75+ years, the total female population in the world reached 62,135,860. However, the total male population is the least 34,941,743.

Throughout the previous 10 years, the number of suicides was generally lower in most countries. There is no colour shift for Poland, Germany and United Kingdom. In 2005, Russia was the country with the highest number of suicides.

The suicide rate for both sexes between the ages of 35 and 54 is greater than the rate for other ages. Although typically lower in 2005, the suicide rate has been rising over time.

Most countries have lower GDP and suicide rate values. However, a few nations with GDPs over $80,000 also have lower suicide rates.
